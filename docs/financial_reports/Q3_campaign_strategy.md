# Q3 Term Deposit Campaign Strategy
## FinSight Bank — Internal Campaign Brief

---

## Overview

Due to recent fluctuations in the Euribor 3-month rate, our focus for Q3 is on securing long-term deposits. Elevated Euribor levels make term deposits competitively priced relative to savings accounts, increasing client receptivity to locking in a guaranteed rate. Campaign messaging should emphasise security, guaranteed returns, and the Deposit Guarantee Scheme coverage up to €100,000.

---

## Target Demographics

1. **Retirees (age 60+):** Historical data shows individuals over 60 are highly receptive to term deposits during times of negative employment variation. Focus messaging on security and guaranteed returns. This segment shows 1.6× the campaign average conversion rate during economic uncertainty.

2. **Previous Campaign Successes:** Any client who subscribed to a previous product (`poutcome = 'success'`) should be fast-tracked into the highest-priority call tier. They have a **4× higher conversion rate** than clients with no prior campaign contact. Assign these leads to senior agents with the strongest close rates.

---

## Guidelines

- Do not target individuals with an `unknown` credit default status — this creates regulatory complications during the suitability assessment and consistently produces below-average conversion.
- Calls made on **Tuesdays and Wednesdays** historically have a 12% higher connection rate than the campaign average. Schedule priority leads for these days in the 10:00–11:30 window.
- Avoid calling blue-collar workers during standard working hours (09:00–17:00); prefer **evening slots (17:30–20:00)** when these clients are more reachable and receptive.
- Maximum **3 contacts per client per 30-day period** in line with Banco de Portugal Instruction 14/2021. Do not override this limit regardless of model score.
- Agents must verbally disclose the APR and the 14-day cooling-off period on every call where a product offer is made (MiFID II requirement).
